## Summary of Document Content

### 1. Technical Report - Rapadura Stream Bridge (SEI 6022.2020/0002811-3 / pg. 1-23)
This report, dated **October 20, 2020**, by **TGPO Engenharia**, details anomalies found in the **Rapadura Stream Bridge** at **Avenida Conselheiro Carrão, No. 1670, Vila Carrão, São Paulo**.

#### 1.1. Inspection Synopsis
*   **Conditions:** Latent instability, ruptured piles in abutment walls, recent but corrected subsidence on the downstream side of the roadway, and undermining at the base of retaining walls.
*   **Evidence:** Excessive deformation in deck slabs, cracks in pile cap beams, severe oxidation in pre-molded slab panels with significant deflections, horizontal crack in the right bank retaining wall, and undermining exposing piles.

#### 1.2. Structural Characteristics
*   The structure uses two types of construction from different periods.
*   **Central portion (under roadway):** Metal profiles (steel plate web, riveted angle iron flanges) and concrete slab on reinforced concrete walls over concrete piles. Designed for **TB 24** load.
*   **Lateral portions (sidewalks):** Pre-molded concrete panels on pile cap beams and pre-molded concrete plates for abutment containment. Sidewalks are approximately **3.90 m** (odd side) and **5.50 m** (even side) wide. Older Google images suggest sidewalks were slightly raised, increasing load on already deflected slabs.

#### 1.3. Initial Diagnosis
*   **Roadway Deck:** Metal beams are in good condition with slight oxidation; reinforced concrete slabs are good.
*   **Concrete Walls:** Undermining exposes foundation piles; right bank wall has a large horizontal crack with infiltration.
*   **Sidewalk Sections:** Ruptured pile heads, cracks in pile cap beams, exposed and severely oxidized rebar in piles and pre-molded slabs (which show excessive deflection), and significant infiltration.
*   **Upstream Containment:** Pre-molded concrete piles with cap beams and plates are damaged, with broken concrete and exposed, oxidized rebar.
*   **Downstream Containment:** Left bank has similar anomalies. Right bank uses gabion boxes without PVC protection, showing oxidation, broken mesh, and material loss, causing voids and instability.
*   **Flow Section:** Insufficient for a **100-year return period** flow, leading to frequent flooding of the avenue during heavy rains.

#### 1.4. Initial Measures and Technical Opinion
*   **TGPO Engenharia** recommends immediate intervention due to latent instability, damage to support piles, insufficient flow section, and heavy traffic.
*   **Recommended Actions:**
    *   Partial closure of **Avenida Conselheiro Carrão** with signage.
    *   Topographic survey and percussion soundings.
    *   Material, teams, and equipment for immediate replacement of the existing structure with a new one designed for a **100-year return period** flow and **TB 45** load.
    *   Two-stage replacement to avoid full road closure, extending canalization by approximately **25.00m** upstream and downstream to protect adjacent properties without expropriation.
*   **Conclusion:** The structure is in a state of "latent instability heading towards collapse," requiring urgent replacement to ensure safety.

### 2. SIURB/OBRAS Information (SEI 6022.2020/0002811-3 / pg. 26-27)
Dated **October 22, 2020**, **Nilton Laganà Junior**, Superintendent of Road Works, forwards the **TGPO Engenharia** report to the Chief Prosecutor. He highlights the critical instability and recommends the immediate replacement of the bridge. **TALUDE CONSTRUÇÕES S.A.** is indicated for the work, having expressed agreement (doc sei **034708227**) and being deemed capable with available resources. The direct contracting is based on **Federal Law No. 8.666/93, Article 24, Item IV**.

### 3. SIURB/ATAJ Legal Opinion (SEI 6022.2020/0002811-3 / pg. 30-32)
Dated **October 23, 2020**, **Paulo César Nannini**, Municipal Prosecutor, reviews the request for emergency replacement. He concurs with the technical assessment of imminent risk and the need for urgent action, justifying direct contracting under **Article 24, Item IV, of Law No. 8.666/93**. He emphasizes that the emergency contract must be limited to the minimum necessary to mitigate risk and that **TALUDE CONSTRUÇÕES S.A.** is suitable due to its registration with **SIURB** (doc. SEI **034763085**) and capacity. He also stresses the need for the contractor to meet fiscal and legal requirements and for the price to be reasonable.

### 4. Authorizing Dispatch (SEI 6022.2020/0002811-3 / pg. 33-34)
Dated **October 26, 2020**, **Vitor Aly**, Municipal Secretary of **SIURB**, authorizes the emergency contracting of **TALUDE CONSTRUÇÕES S.A.** (CNPJ/MF **14.914.786/0001-67**) for the bridge replacement, for a period of up to **180 days**, based on the technical report (doc. SEI **034703066**) and the legal opinion (doc. SEI **034763912**). He mandates publication, issuance of commitment for expenses, pricing based on **SIURB** unit values and market research, and revalidation of the contractor's legal and fiscal aptitude.

### 7. Pathology Report (SEI 6022.2020/0002811-3 / pg. 41-129)
This report, dated **November 2020**, by **TGPO Engenharia**, details the inspection activities, geometry, and pathologies of the bridge, following **NBR 9452/2019**.
*   **Location:** **Avenida Conselheiro Carrão, No. 1670, Vila Carrão, SP**.
*   **Description:** The bridge has a total width of **20.74 m** and a length of **5.50 m**. It consists of an original central section (10.01 m wide) with mixed metal-concrete structure (metal I-beams, concrete slab) on cyclopean concrete walls over wooden piles, and two widened sections (7.70 m upstream, 3.03 m downstream) with pre-molded concrete slabs on pre-molded concrete piles.
*   **Anomalies:**
    *   **Platform:** Adequate, but lacks protection barriers.
    *   **Pavement:** Generally good, but a small area on the right bank shows subsidence and cracking due to sub-base pumping.
    *   **Superstructure:** Original section (metal beams) is good, but its wooden pile foundations are exposed to air-water variations, leading to decay. Widened sections have pre-molded slabs with excessive deflection, exposed and oxidized rebar, concrete segregation, and broken sections.
    *   **Containment:** Original cyclopean concrete walls have horizontal cracks and are supported by decaying wooden piles. Widened sections' pre-molded pile containments show undermining and material loss. Gabion boxes on the right bank are corroded and failing.
    *   **Drainage:** Inadequate, leading to flooding.
    *   **Guardrails:** Damaged with exposed and oxidized rebar.
    *   **Expansion Joints:** Non-existent.
*   **Classification (NBR 9452/2019):**
    *   **Structural:** 1 (Deep anomalies, deformations above norms).
    *   **Functional:** 2 (No expansion joints, damaged sidewalks/guardrails).
    *   **Durability:** 1 (Pavement issues, but not impacting overall structure).
*   **Conclusion:** Due to severe deficiencies in foundations, superstructure, and inadequate flow section, the report recommends replacing the entire structure with one designed for **TB 450** load and a **100-year return period** flow.

### 8. Calculation Memory (SEI 6022.2020/0002811-3 / pg. 130-211)
This document, dated **December 2020**, by **TGPO Engenharia**, provides the structural calculation justification for the new bridge design.
*   **Objective:** Justify the geometric and structural characteristics of the new bridge.
*   **Characteristics of New Structure:** Pre-molded concrete slabs (**20 cm** thick, **6.66 m** long) with a **20 cm** cast-in-place concrete topping. Roadway has variable **0-6 cm** binder and **5 cm** asphalt pavement. Sidewalks have **25 cm** regularization layer and **1.20 m x 0.20 m** parapet.
*   **Meso-structure:** Polygonal crossbeam transferring loads to **41 cm** diameter root piles, covered with shotcrete.
*   **Channel Bottom:** A **25 cm** thick slab **2.75 m** below the upper slab prevents pile displacement.
*   **Load Design:** Designed for **TB-450** mobile load according to **NBR-7188**.
*   **Materials:** Concrete classes **C20** and **C30**, **CA-50** steel.
*   **Geotechnical Data:** Includes SPT (Standard Penetration Test) results from boreholes **SP-02** and **SP-03**, showing varying soil conditions and load capacities for the piles.
